Security Guides

Strong passwords are your first line of defense against unauthorized access. Follow these best practices:

  • Use at least 12 characters with a mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers and symbols
  • Avoid common words, phrases, or personal information
  • Never reuse passwords across different accounts
  • Change passwords immediately if a service reports a breach
  • Consider using passphrases (e.g., "CorrectHorseBatteryStaple")

Phishing is when attackers try to trick you into revealing sensitive information. Watch for these signs:

Suspicious Elements
  • Urgent or threatening language
  • Generic greetings ("Dear Customer")
  • Misspellings and poor grammar
  • Mismatched sender addresses
Verification Steps
  • Hover over links to see actual URL
  • Check sender's email address carefully
  • Contact the company directly
  • Look for HTTPS and padlock icon
Example Phishing Email:

From: support@micros0ft.com (notice the zero)

Subject: Urgent! Your account will be suspended

Dear User,

We detected unusual activity on your account. Click here to verify your identity immediately or your account will be closed.

Microsoft Support Team

A Virtual Private Network (VPN) encrypts your internet connection to protect your data. Key benefits:

Public Wi-Fi Safety

Encrypts traffic on coffee shop, airport, and hotel networks

Location Privacy

Hides your real IP address and location

Secure Access

Safely connect to company resources from anywhere

VPN Best Practices:
  • Always connect to VPN before accessing work resources
  • Use company-approved VPN software
  • Disconnect when not in use
  • Never use free VPN services for work
  • Report any connection issues immediately

Your devices need protection whether you're working from home or traveling:

Essential Protections
  • Enable full-disk encryption
  • Install and update antivirus software
  • Enable firewall protection
  • Use device tracking (Find My Device)
Physical Security
  • Never leave devices unattended in public
  • Use privacy screens in public spaces
  • Secure devices at home if you have roommates
  • Enable remote wipe capability
